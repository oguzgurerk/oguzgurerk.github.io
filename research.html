Research Projects

Outlier Analysis under Heavy Tails

As a senior project, under the supervision of Prof. Ümit Işlak, I worked on outlier analysis under heavy tails and multivariate generalizations of types of outliers. This was mainly motivated by the fact that in statistics, though the term outlier is often articulated in various fields, a consensus for a mathematical definition for an outlier has not been established yet. The purpose was to investigate and to propose definitions of types of outliers so that we can minimize the number of data/domain-specific parameters for the working definition to have qualities that we expect from a rigorous mathematical definition to possess. I worked on $a^2 + b^2 = c^k \in \mathbb{D}$ both discrete and continuous problems concerning outlier detection; the problem of detecting anomalies in discrete/symbolic sequences where several anomaly detection techniques use the length of the longest common subsequence as a similarity measure, and for the continuous case, our approach was based on the distances between different order statistics, and employing moving averages to investigate the tail behaviors of hypothetical and/or empirical distributions in a more refined way.

A Study on Permutation Graphs (joint with Ümit Işlak and Mehmet Akif Yıldız)

For a given permutation πn in Sn, a random permutation graph is formed by including an edge between two vertices i and j if and only if $(i - j)(\pi_n(i) - \pi_n (j)) < 0$. In this paper, we study various statistics of random permutation graphs. In particular, we prove central limit theorems for the number m-cliques and cycles of size at least m. Other problems of interest are on the number of isolated vertices, the distribution of a given node (the mid-node as a special case) and extremal degree statistics. Besides, we introduce a directed version of random permutation graphs, and provide two distinct paths that provide variations/generalizations of the model discussed in this paper.
